How to Play

Hectoc: A Mental Challenge

Hectoc is a Mental Calculation game, developed by Yusnier Viera in the 2010s.

Each question presents a sequence of 6 digits, each ranging from 1 to 9.

Example: 249384

The aim is to use these numbers in order to find the number 100.

Here are two solutions to this example:

2 × (49 + 3 – 8/4) = 100
(2 - 4 + 9 + 3)^(8/4) = 100

Allowed Operations:

• Combining adjacent numbers (e.g., 4 and 9 to make 49).
• Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
• Negative symbols.
• Powers (e.g., ‘^’).
• Brackets for order of operations.

Restrictions:

• Numbers cannot be rearranged.
• No other symbols (e.g., square roots) are allowed.
• All numbers must be used.

Hectoc in Competitions

Hectoc has been featured in advanced international mental calculation competitions, such as the JMCWC and Memoriad.

Strategies for Success

Remember, most hectocs have multiple solutions. Work backwards from 100!

Useful 100 Combinations:

• 10 × 10
• 99 + 1
• 25 × 4

Strategy A: Starting Near 100

If a number is close to 100, adjust the rest.
Example: 1 + 96 + (2 × 6)/4 = 100

Strategy B: 100 = 10²

Build 2 and 10.
Example: (1 + (9 – 6)²)^(6 – 4) = 100

Strategy C: 100 = 10 × 10

Example: (1 + 9) × (6 + 2 + 6 – 4) = 100

Strategy D: 100 = 20 × 5

Example: (–1^9 + 6) × 2 × (6 + 4) = 100

Final Thoughts

Use these strategies and think backwards from 100 for quicker solutions!
